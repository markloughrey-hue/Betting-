CRED's value proposition to the betting industry

Two ways CRED moves the needle for operators.

Both come back to the same edge: external context on the people operators already hold. One is live today — enriching first-party data to reveal who their bettors really are. One is in build — mapping the sports-rights market so buyers stop overpaying. Below is each, with what operators told us directly in discovery.

Pillar 1 · Live today In market — testing with operators now

Enrich first-party data → understand every bettor

Operators know a customer as a name and an email. They don't know what they earn, where they work, or what they actually follow. CRED matches their first-party list against 900M individual profiles and hands back income, affluence and sport-affinity context — so they target the players worth targeting and get ahead of affordability regulation.

The honest part — and where the value really sits

A CVM model on top of this isn't proprietary — a sophisticated operator can build their own (and the big ones already have). We can hand them a plug-and-play one to get started, but that's the packaging. The value is the external context on their punters — the part no operator can generate from their own four walls.

One caution operators flagged

Predicted NGR per right is the hard part — "there's no proof Serie A earns you X." Lead with ownership & expiry (the defensible facts); treat NGR estimates as a guided add-on, not a promise.

What the market told us it's worth

Pricing signal — straight from operators

Rights mapping
€5–10k / month
Quoted independently by both MaxBet and PalmsBet for multi-provider operators — "easily," against savings in the hundreds of thousands. Tier-1 operators ("the monsters") would pay more.
Nuance from PalmsBet: smaller books won't sign a 2-year deal — they'll pay for short, intense access during a 4–6 week supplier-decision window. Build year-round value for the big buyers.
Data enrichment
Per match
Price on volume of records matched, not a flat fee — depth-tiered (income band cheap; full profile + sport affinity premium). Recurring feeds (e.g. sportsbet.io's 500K/month) move to committed-volume subscription.
Reference point: enrichment engagements like Aston Martin (~£20k) anchor the data-side value; every operator wants a free sample test first.
